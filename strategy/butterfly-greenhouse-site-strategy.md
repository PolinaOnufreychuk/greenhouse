# Butterfly Greenhouse — Website Strategy & Information Architecture

VenGreenery brief: a new permanent indoor attraction (tropical greenhouse, 500 butterflies, exotic animals) expanding the VenGreenery ecosystem. This document is the structural and creative brief for the website — information architecture, section-by-section direction, interaction/motion ideas, photography and brand-graphic integration. It does not contain final copy: **final copy will be written in Ukrainian**; the direction notes below describe angle and tone only, in English, as a brief for the copywriter.

Research grounding: immersive storytelling sites perform best as a scroll-driven narrative arc (beginning → middle → end) rather than a hierarchy of feature cards; each section should read as a "scene" that pulls the visitor into the next one; interactive/immersive sites see materially higher scroll depth than static ones; and attraction sites specifically benefit from collapsing practical/booking information into one low-friction "plan your visit" moment rather than scattering logistics through the narrative. This shaped the decision to keep the homepage to 8 tight scenes rather than a longer list of thin sections.

---

## 1. Information Architecture

- **Primary surface:** one-page storytelling homepage — carries ~90% of the narrative and conversion weight.
- **Secondary pages** (utility/reference, not narrative): **Tickets**, **Visit Info**, **FAQ**, **Gallery**.
- **Navigation:** persistent, unobtrusive top nav; a sticky "Get Tickets" CTA appears once the visitor scrolls past the hero.
- **Depth:** flat — every secondary page and the ticket flow is one click away from anywhere on the site.
- **Cross-linking:** the homepage's "Plan Your Visit" section (§2.7) is the hub that hands off to Tickets and Visit Info; Gallery and FAQ are reachable from nav/footer, not narrated inline.

## 2. Homepage — Section-by-Section

### 2.1 Hero — "Step Into the Wings"
**Purpose:** first impression — establish mood instantly, not information.
**Copy angle:** one short emotional line, no subhead stacking. Direction: evoke the moment of stepping through the door into humid tropical air and the first butterfly crossing your path. Never "Visit our butterfly attraction!" — always sensory, present-tense, second person.
**Interaction:** cursor-following (or scroll-triggered) butterfly drifting across the hero art; subtle parallax on foliage layers behind the headline.
**Motion:** slow, ambient entrance choreography (fade + drift, not slide-ins); looping full-bleed video or cinemagraph of live butterflies preferred over static photo. Hold back on anything flashy here — this section's job is calm immersion, not spectacle.
**Photography:** full-bleed, macro-adjacent (close enough to feel the wings, wide enough to feel the space). Slight color grade toward the brand palette rather than raw photo tones.
**Brand graphics:** minimal here — one or two outlined illustration accents at the frame edges at most; let photography lead.

### 2.2 The Invitation
**Purpose:** answer "why should I visit?" and "what makes it unique?" together — this is not a features list, it's an emotional pitch that also plants the positioning (permanent, indoor, part of VenGreenery).
**Copy angle:** short, curious, inviting — frames the greenhouse as a new chapter of the VenGreenery story ("the flowers you know, now with wings" territory — copywriter's call), not a separate brand.
**Interaction:** opening-doors / parting-leaves reveal transition arriving from the Hero — the section "opens" rather than cuts in.
**Motion:** organic curved wipe or mask reveal; text arrives with a soft stagger, not a hard fade.
**Photography:** one strong environmental photo (wide shot of the interior) paired with a cut-out butterfly or floral sticker breaking the frame edge.
**Brand graphics:** floral graphic elements and one colorful sticker-style label (e.g. "500 butterflies" as a badge, not a bullet point) — this is a good place to let illustration overlap photography for the first time.

### 2.3 The Experience Walkthrough
**Purpose:** the narrative spine — answers "what will I actually experience?" by simulating the physical walk through the greenhouse (entrance → canopy → wetlands → nursery → exit).
**Copy angle:** editorial, present-tense, scene-by-scene — each beat reads like a paragraph from a nature magazine feature, not a room-by-room brochure.
**Interaction:** horizontal or panel-based scroll-jacked sequence, one "room" per panel; hover or scroll-triggered reveals for small details (a hidden butterfly, a plant label) within each scene.
**Motion:** scroll-linked parallax between foreground plants/butterflies and background photography; transitions between panels should feel like walking forward, not paging — organic easing, no hard cuts.
**Photography:** alternating full-bleed environment shots and macro insets; strict alternating left/right visual rhythm as the visitor scrolls to avoid monotony.
**Brand graphics:** outlined illustration "path" motif or footprints/wing-trail connecting panels; decorative stickers marking each "stop" on the walk.

### 2.4 The Living World — Butterflies, Animals & Plants
**Purpose:** answers "what animals and butterflies will I see?" — one curated cast-of-characters moment rather than two separate catalog sections.
**Copy angle:** introduce species as characters with personality/story, not a spec sheet (no Latin-name-first taxonomy dump). Copywriter should pick a handful of "signature" species/animals to feature by name and voice; rest can live in Gallery.
**Interaction:** hover-to-reveal cards with a wing-flutter or wing-open micro-interaction; cursor-following butterfly reappears here, now interactive (visitor can "chase" it to reveal a species card).
**Motion:** staggered entrance as the section scrolls into view; playful but restrained — this is the most "delightful" section on the page, so it's the right place to spend the motion budget, but each element should settle quickly rather than loop indefinitely.
**Photography:** mix of real macro photography (species close-ups) and cut-out treatments so photographed butterflies feel like they're layered into the illustrated world, not sitting in a photo frame.
**Brand graphics:** heaviest use of floating decorative butterflies, floral frames around photo cut-outs, and colorful labels for species names — this section is the clearest opportunity to blend photography and illustration called for in the brief.

### 2.5 Look Inside — Architecture & Atmosphere
**Purpose:** answers "how does the greenhouse look?" — the structure itself as a design object (light, glass, humidity, sound), a sensory beat between the "cast" section and the reassurance section.
**Copy angle:** short, atmospheric, almost architectural-magazine in tone — describe light through glass, warm air, ambient sound, rather than repeating "experience" language already used earlier.
**Interaction:** light/glass shader-like hover effect (e.g. a soft glare or refraction that follows cursor movement across a glass-structure photo); optional subtle ambient sound toggle (bird calls / water) if the site supports audio.
**Motion:** slow depth-of-field style parallax; restrained — this section should feel like a breath, contrasting with the busier Living World section before it.
**Photography:** large-format architectural/interior photography, wide compositions with generous negative space.
**Brand graphics:** very light touch — this is a "quiet" section; at most a single outlined illustration accent (e.g. a leaf sketch) as a divider into the next section.

### 2.6 Made for Families
**Purpose:** answers "is this suitable for families?" — reassurance on accessibility, safety, pacing, age-range, without reading like a policy page.
**Copy angle:** warm, practical-but-not-corporate — reassurance framed as an invitation ("built for little explorers and their grown-ups" territory — copywriter's call), not a liability disclaimer.
**Interaction:** light — icon-style reveal-on-scroll for accessibility/pacing points rather than a stock icon grid; consider a small illustrated family silhouette walking the same "path" motif used in §2.3 for continuity.
**Motion:** gentle fade/stagger only; this section should read fast and put the visitor at ease, not slow them down with heavy animation.
**Photography:** real photography of families/children in the space (candid, not posed-stock feeling) — this is the section most in need of authentic, non-stock imagery.
**Brand graphics:** playful but restrained stickers (e.g. an age-range badge, a "stroller & wheelchair friendly" label styled as a sticker rather than a plain icon).

### 2.7 Plan Your Visit
**Purpose:** the conversion section — merges the practical teaser (hours, duration, location) with the ticket call-to-action into one low-friction beat; hands off to the Tickets and Visit Info pages.
**Copy angle:** direct and confident, shortest copy on the page — this is the moment urgency and clarity matter more than atmosphere. Still warm, not corporate.
**Interaction:** ticket-type teaser (e.g. Family / Individual / Group) as sticker-style badges that link out; sticky CTA from nav reinforces here rather than introducing a new pattern.
**Motion:** minimal — this section should feel stable and easy to act on, not busy. Any motion here should support scanability (e.g. a subtle highlight on the primary CTA), not decorate.
**Photography:** one warm, inviting photo (e.g. visitors exiting smiling, golden light) rather than another wide interior shot — this section's photography job is emotional closure + trust, not new information.
**Brand graphics:** ticket-badge stickers, a colorful "Book Now" label treatment consistent with the identity guide's decorative label style.

### 2.8 Closing — Return to VenGreenery
**Purpose:** closes the loop, reconnecting the Greenhouse back to the broader VenGreenery park ecosystem; soft CTA + footer.
**Copy angle:** brief, warm sign-off that reminds the visitor this is one wing of a larger world worth exploring (park-wide cross-sell without hard-selling).
**Interaction:** minimal — footer-level; perhaps a final small butterfly drifting off-screen as a closing motif, echoing the Hero.
**Motion:** simple fade; this is the resting point of the page, no scroll-jacking or parallax.
**Photography:** small — a wide VenGreenery park shot or a composite linking greenhouse imagery back to the park's flower fields.
**Brand graphics:** VenGreenery pattern/motif as a footer texture, tying the sub-brand visually back to the parent park.

## 3. Secondary Pages

Each secondary page stays "in world" (same brand graphics, typography, restrained motion) but is utilitarian and scannable — not narrative-driven like the homepage.

- **Tickets:** ticket types, pricing, timed-entry selection, add-ons. Structured for fast scanning and completion, not storytelling; brand graphics used as visual anchors (badges per ticket tier) rather than decoration.
- **Visit Info:** hours, location/directions, accessibility details, what to bring, house rules — organized as a short scannable reference, echoing the tone of §2.6 without repeating its copy.
- **FAQ:** grouped by visitor concern (before you visit / accessibility / tickets & booking / what to expect inside); accordion pattern, no heavy motion.
- **Gallery:** the overflow home for species/photography not featured on the homepage's Living World section (§2.4); masonry or editorial grid with lightbox, still using cut-out/sticker accents at the edges rather than a plain grid.

---

## 4. Global Motion & Interaction Principles

- Prefer organic, curved transitions (mask reveals, wipes, parting-leaves effects) over hard cuts or straight fades.
- Keep a restrained parallax depth budget — 2–3 layers max per section; more reads as noisy, not premium.
- Reuse one or two signature interaction motifs across the whole page (the cursor-following/chaseable butterfly; the "path" motif from §2.3 recurring in §2.6) so the site feels coherent rather than a demo reel of unrelated effects.
- Provide a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback for every scroll-jacked or parallax section — static, well-composed layouts should be the fallback, not a broken experience.
- Performance guardrails: this is a photography-heavy site — lazy-load offscreen media, compress and serve responsive image sizes, and never let decorative motion block or throttle scroll performance.

## 5. Verification Checklist

- [ ] Every visitor-journey question from the brief (why visit / what's unique / what will I experience / what will I see / what does it look like / is it family-friendly / how do I buy tickets) is answered by exactly one homepage section.
- [ ] No section relies on a generic card-grid pattern.
- [ ] Every homepage section has copy angle + interaction + motion + photography + brand-graphic guidance — no gaps.
- [ ] Tone stays warm/curious/magical/family-friendly/premium throughout — never childish, never corporate — confirmed at copywriting stage in Ukrainian.
